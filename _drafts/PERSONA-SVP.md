## Distribution Strategy: Three Channels, Three Audiences

The SVP persona needs to reach different audiences through different channels. Each channel
has a distinct role.

| Channel | Role | Audience | Content Length |
|---------|------|----------|---------------|
| **Blog** (mcgarrah.org) | Technical source of truth | Engineers, architects, hiring managers doing deep diligence | Full technical depth |
| **Substack** (mcgarrah.substack.com) | Long-form leadership home | Leadership audience, newsletter subscribers, persistent archive | 1500-3000 words, strategic framing |
| **LinkedIn** | Amplifier + hook | 500+ connections: former colleagues, recruiters, hiring committees | 300-800 words, drives to Substack |

### How the Channels Reinforce Each Other

- **Blog** is the technical source of truth. Every hands-on article lives here permanently.
  This is where the "still builds things" credibility lives. When a hiring committee does
  deep technical diligence, this is what they find.
- **Substack** is the long-form leadership home. The retrospective newsletters bundle blog
  posts with strategic framing. The standalone leadership articles live here as the
  persistent, linkable, shareable versions. Substack grows slowly but compounds — it's
  the archive that outlasts any single LinkedIn post's feed visibility. Substack is where
  the SVP-voiced content *lives*.
- **LinkedIn** is the amplifier. Short posts (300-800 words) that distill the key insight
  from a Substack article into a bite-sized hook, then link to the full Substack piece.
  LinkedIn's value is reach into your 500+ professional network — former colleagues who
  can vouch for the work, recruiters sourcing for SVP roles, and hiring managers evaluating
  candidates. LinkedIn posts are ephemeral in the feed; Substack articles are permanent.

### The LinkedIn Advantage: Reach Into Your Network

With 500+ connections who are former colleagues, managers, and industry peers, LinkedIn is
where the SVP persona has immediate reach. A Substack article that nobody reads doesn't
move the needle. But a LinkedIn post that hooks 3-5 former colleagues into reading the
full Substack piece — and commenting "I saw Michael build this at [company]" — is worth
more than 10,000 anonymous page views.

LinkedIn properties that matter for SVP positioning:

- **Social proof is visible.** Colleague comments become public endorsements.
- **Recruiters live there.** Executive recruiters sourcing for SVP/Head of AI search LinkedIn.
- **The network effect is immediate.** Your connections see the post; their connections see
  their engagement.
- **Posts persist on your profile.** LinkedIn articles/posts become part of your professional
  profile visible to anyone who views it.

### Publication Flow

For retrospective newsletters (bundling blog posts):

```
Blog posts (technical depth, published over weeks)
    ↓
Substack retrospective (long-form strategic bundle, mid-month)
    ↓
LinkedIn post (short hook + link to Substack)
```

For standalone leadership pieces:

```
Substack article (long-form leadership content, the canonical version)
    ↓
LinkedIn post (300-800 word distillation + link to Substack)
    ↓
Blog reference post (optional, only if technical companion piece warrants it)
```

Substack hosts the full article. LinkedIn drives traffic to it.
